Traditional Foods of Andhra Pradesh

Pesarattu

Pesarattu is a protein-rich dosa made from green gram (moong dal). It is crisp on the outside and soft on the inside. Often served with ginger chutney and sometimes upma (MLA Pesarattu). It’s a popular breakfast in coastal Andhra regions. Healthy, filling, and quick to make.

Pulihora

Pulihora is a tangy tamarind rice seasoned with spices. Ingredients include mustard seeds, curry leaves, chilies, and peanuts. It is commonly prepared during festivals and as temple prasadam. The sourness of tamarind and spice blend makes it addictive. Simple yet flavorful, it’s a comfort dish in many homes.

Andhra Chicken Curry

This spicy chicken curry defines Andhra’s bold flavor profile. It uses a rich mix of onions, red chilies, and garam masala. The curry is thick, fiery, and aromatic. Often served with rice, pulao, or chapati. A must-try for lovers of hot and spicy food.

Gongura Pachadi

Gongura, or sorrel leaves, are a signature ingredient in Andhra food. The chutney is made with green/red chilies, garlic, and mustard seeds. It’s sour, spicy, and has a unique earthy flavor. Typically eaten with steamed rice and a dollop of ghee. Gongura is also used in dal and meat preparations.

Ariselu

Ariselu is a traditional Andhra sweet made during festivals. Prepared with rice flour and jaggery, and deep-fried in ghee. The texture is crispy on the outside and soft inside. Often topped with sesame seeds for added crunch. Especially popular during Sankranti celebrations.
