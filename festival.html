Festivals of Andhra Pradesh

Ugadi

Ugadi marks the Telugu New Year and is celebrated with joy and devotion.
People decorate their homes with mango leaves and create colorful rangolis.
Traditional dishes like Ugadi Pachadi symbolize different emotions of life.
It's a day to start fresh, wearing new clothes and visiting temples.
The festival is deeply rooted in Telugu culture and traditions.

Sankranti

Sankranti is a harvest festival celebrated with great enthusiasm in Andhra Pradesh.
It lasts for four days: Bhogi, Sankranti, Kanuma, and Mukkanuma.
People fly kites, light bonfires, and enjoy sweets like Ariselu and Pongal.
Farmers thank the Sun God for a good harvest.
The festival brings families together in rural and urban areas alike.

Dasara (Vijayadashami)

Dasara, also known as Vijayadashami, celebrates the victory of good over evil.
In Vijayawada, the Kanaka Durga Temple is the central place of worship.
The festival spans nine days of Navaratri followed by grand celebrations.
Devotees participate in cultural events, processions, and rituals.
It's one of the most vibrant and colorful festivals in Andhra Pradesh.

Diwali

Diwali, the festival of lights, is celebrated with lamps, sweets, and fireworks.
Homes are decorated with diyas and rangoli, symbolizing the triumph of light.
Families come together to exchange gifts and blessings.
Children enjoy bursting crackers and lighting sparklers.
The festival brings positivity, hope, and joy to all households.

Krishna Pushkaralu

Krishna Pushkaralu is a sacred river festival held every 12 years.
Devotees gather on the banks of the Krishna River to take holy dips.
It is believed to cleanse sins and bring spiritual blessings.
The festival includes rituals, prayers, and cultural activities.
Cities like Vijayawada witness lakhs of pilgrims during this time.
